Slides 		‚ÄúNew direction research for parabolic equations‚Äù
Prof. Hisao Fujita Yashima (NHSM, Algeria)
In the first part, we recall classical methods for the study of partial differential equations, such as the method of characteristics for solving transport equation, the fundamental solution of heat equation, the principle of maximum, the solutions in Sobolev spaces, the estimation of the Holder continuity, the stochastic representation of the solution of a parabolic equation. We are going to see also a simple case of the convergence of the solution of a parabolic equation to the solution of a transport equation. In the second part, we illustrate the specific method of the new research orientation for parabolic equations that we propose. This method is based on the approximate solutions constructed by using the fundamental solution of the heat equation. The utility of this approach is that the approximation by these approximate solutions permits us to prove the convergence of the solution of parabolic equation to the solution of transport equation when the coefficient of diffusion tends to 0, as our intuition for physical phenomena of transport and diffusion suggests. We present some results obtained in this orientation of research and indicate what we can expect in the development of this research.
